Voice and Multimodal

Converts between audio, video, and text to enable voice and multimodal interaction

Real World Analogy

Like a simultaneous interpreter at the United Nations. The interpreter listens in one language, processes the meaning, and speaks in another language in near real time. The challenge is not understanding or speaking, it is doing both fast enough that the conversation feels natural.

How It Works

Voice and multimodal harnesses let you talk to AI naturally using your voice and get spoken responses back. The system converts your speech to text, sends it to the AI for processing, and converts the response back into speech. More advanced systems skip the text step entirely, processing audio directly for faster conversations.

Latency is the defining constraint. In a voice conversation, anything over about 800 milliseconds starts to feel awkward. Every component must be optimized for speed. The best systems begin speaking before the full response is generated, streaming audio as soon as the first words are ready.

Emotion detection represents the frontier. Systems like Hume AI analyze not just what you say but how you say it, detecting frustration, confusion, or happiness from your tone. The AI then adjusts its response accordingly, creating interactions that feel fundamentally different from emotionally flat voice assistants.

Architecture

Components

  • Speech to text pipelines with noise reduction and language detection
  • Text to speech engines with voice cloning and natural prosody
  • Real time streaming infrastructure for sub second response times
  • Turn taking management for natural conversation flow
  • Emotion detection from voice tone, pace, and volume
  • Multimodal fusion combining audio, video, and text inputs

Key Terms

ASR

Automatic Speech Recognition. Technology that converts spoken words into text.

TTS

Text to Speech. Technology that converts written text into natural sounding spoken audio.

Affect aware interaction

AI that detects and responds to user emotions, adjusting both what it says and how it says it.
